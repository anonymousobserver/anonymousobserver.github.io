The beginning

The quote above is from the series "Person of Interest", season 4 episode 11, where one of the protagonists, Harold Finch, is teaching the "Machine" to play chess. And I have been thinking about it for a while, in the context of this blog (or whatever you think this is). It's been almost a month since I started looking for ways to create a blog and even more since I first thought of writing one. Why is it so difficult to start something new, something afresh? and, taking the analogy from the quote, if there are so many possibilities and chances to fix the mistakes, why is it so difficult to take the first step?

A contrasting view is from an unrelated topic - energy landscape sampling - in which where you start (initial configuration) is important. A really bad starting point (like a local minima trap in the landscape), can negatively affect the outcome (in terms of sampling efficiency). Theoretically, there is always a non-zero probability of going from point A to B in the landscape. The limiting factor here is the time. Will this starting point lead to the desired outcome in finite time? Not only that, but will it take you to that outcome through a favourable path?

Both the concerns are valid. One can't afford to wait forever to start, so one has to start somewhere. But, the somewhere can't be anywhere, it has to be good enough. And this seems like good enough to me, so here I am.
